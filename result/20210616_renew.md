### 3. Malware and Attack Behavior per Host
The incident consists of three distinct infections occurring on different workstations:

*   **Host 10.6.15.119 (Tommy Vega):** This host acted as the initial entry point. It exhibited a multi-stage infection involving:
    *   **Download:** A PE executable was downloaded from `8.209.119.208`.
    *   **C2/Trojan:** Established Cobalt Strike beaconing to `65.60.35.141` and Hancitor/Tordal check-ins to `194.226.60.15`.
    *   **Stealer:** Exhibited Ficker Stealer activity communicating with `185.66.15.228`.
*   **Host 10.6.15.187 (Horace Maddox):** This host was infected with **Qbot-style** malware, showing SSL certificate patterns and a JA3 match for **possible Dridex** communicating with `207.246.77.75`.
*   **Host 10.6.15.93 (Raquel Anderson):** This host was infected with **AgentTesla**, which was used to exfiltrate data via SMTP (port 587) to `67.20.116.164`.

*Note: While SMB connections were observed from 10.6.15.119 to the other two workstations, there is no direct evidence of administrative-level exploitation (e.g., service creation or admin share writes) to confirm a single lateral movement chain; therefore, they are treated as independent infections.*

### 5. Anomaly Resolution
*   **Beacons/Exfil Candidates:** All identified beacons (Cobalt Strike, Hancitor, Dridex) and high-ratio exfiltration candidates (Cobalt Strike volume, Ficker Stealer ratio) are directly linked to the malware identified on the three victim hosts.
*   **No DNS Direct IPs:** The list of IPs contacted without DNS queries corresponds to the known C2 infrastructure for Cobalt Strike and Dridex.
*   **Role Deviation:** The deviation on `10.6.15.93` (using port 587/SMTP) is explained by the AgentTesla exfiltration alert.
*   **DNS Anomalies:** High entropy queries for hostnames (e.g., `dekstop-a1ctjvy.saltmobsters.com`) are attributed to standard Active Directory/DHCP behavior in this context.
